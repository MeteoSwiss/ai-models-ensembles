\begin{table}[t]
  \centering
  \small
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{6pt}
  \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.20}
  \caption{Production baselines. All seven models run at 112 init times
           $\times$ 10 members = 1120 forecasts each, then intercompared on
           the same grid, variables, and leads. The three post-hoc
           perturbation baselines use the \winner-marked configuration from
           Tab.~\ref{tab:ablation}; their listed loss is the deterministic
           training loss of the underlying checkpoint (Aurora: Bodnar et al.\
           2025, arXiv:2405.13063, weighted MAE; GraphCast: Lam et al.\ 2023,
           arXiv:2212.12794, latitude- and variable-weighted MSE; SFNO: Bonev
           et al.\ 2023, arXiv:2306.03838, channel-normalised relative
           spherical L2). The trained probabilistic baselines are: AIFS-CRPS
           (Lang et al.\ 2024, arXiv:2412.15832) - a GNN encoder/decoder with
           a sliding-window transformer processor, with per-member noise
           embeddings replacing the standard layer normalisations of every
           processor pre-norm block, trained with an almost-fair CRPS loss;
           NVIDIA Atlas-ERA5 - a latent diffusion Transformer (DiT) sampled
           via the stochastic-interpolant framework of Albergo and
           Vanden-Eijnden (2023), trained by velocity-matching the
           interpolant drift (Chen et al.\ 2024, arXiv:2403.13724; no Atlas
           preprint exists, loss verified from the earth2studio source);
           FourCastNet 3 (Bonev et al.\ 2025, arXiv:2507.12144) - a spherical
           neural operator combining local DISCO and global spectral
           convolutions, with a per-step latent noise variable whose
           evolution on the sphere follows a learned diffusion process,
           trained against a joint spatial-and-spectral CRPS objective. The
           IFS-ENS classical reference is the operational ECMWF ensemble
           (Cy49r1, Nov 2024): a TCo1279L137 (~9 km, 137 levels) primitive-
           equation model integrating from initial conditions perturbed by
           Ensemble of Data Assimilations (EDA) plus 48~h singular vectors,
           with model-error perturbations from the Stochastically Perturbed
           Parametrizations (SPP) scheme (27 uncertain parametrisation
           elements, AR(1) random fields at 1000 + 3000~km and 3 + 30 day
           decorrelation). We subsample 10 of the 50 perturbed members
           (stratified, dropping the control). See ECMWF IFS Documentation
           Cy49r1 Part V.}
